# Leadership Offsite Strategy Slide

You're VP of Strategy preparing a single pivotal slide for the annual leadership offsite, meant to anchor a live debate rather than get talked past.

It needs to lay out, all visible together in the same moment: what the company is genuinely strong on internally right now (category-leading customer satisfaction, a strong balance sheet with 18 months of runway at current burn) and where it's genuinely weak (release cadence has slipped to quarterly when competitors ship monthly, and the European team is thin enough that a single departure would hurt), sitting right next to what's opening up externally (a competitor's recent exit from the mid-market segment leaves real room to move) and what's closing in (a well-funded new entrant just announced a product in the same space, and input costs are climbing faster than pricing can absorb).

The whole point is that the room reacts to all four at once — nothing sequential, nothing revealed one at a time — since the offsite's format is built around live pushback on exactly this slide. Keep every point to a short phrase, not a full sentence — this gets read off the screen while people are talking, not read quietly.
