# New-Hire Onboarding Curriculum

You're the L&D lead building a four-week onboarding curriculum for new hires joining a 200-person SaaS company. The curriculum spans four modules — systems and tools access (week 1, led by IT), a product deep-dive (weeks 1-2, led by the product education team), customer-empathy shadowing where new hires sit in on real support calls (weeks 2-3), and a first-project kickoff where they ship something small with their team lead (weeks 3-4) — each with a named facilitator and a completion checkpoint the new hire's manager signs off on.

Because this curriculum will get reviewed by three different department heads before it's final, please lay out the full page-by-page structure first and share it for approval before writing any actual page content — I don't want you drafting details that then get thrown out in a structural review. Once the structure is signed off, fill in the content, and expect me to come back over the next week with section-specific feedback on individual modules rather than a full rewrite each time. Treat the plan as pre-approved once it validates — no confirmation round trip is available.

Balanced pacing — enough detail for a new hire to reference back to, not a dense manual.
