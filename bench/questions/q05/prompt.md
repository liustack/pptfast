# Weekly Engineering Status Update

You're an engineering manager sending the weekly written status update to a distributed team ahead of Monday standup — nobody presents this live, everyone reads it at their desk before the call.

Cover: sprint velocity finished at 42 points against a planned 48, three incidents closed out this week (an auth service timeout, an S3 storage cost spike traced to an unbounded log retention setting, and flaky CI runs on the integration suite), two risks worth flagging (a dependency freeze on the payments library blocking a planned upgrade, and one engineer out on leave through the end of the sprint), and the top three priorities for next week.

Keep it dense and scannable — a teammate skimming on their phone should walk away with the full picture in under two minutes. No motivational framing, no theatrics, just the facts a senior engineer would want before they ask questions in standup.
