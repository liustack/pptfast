# Q2 Budget Performance Review

You're the regional finance director preparing the Q2 budget performance update for the executive committee's Thursday review. The approved starting budget was $4.2M; actual quarter-end spend landed at $3.65M, and the committee wants to see exactly how the number got there step by step, not just a before-and-after snapshot: marketing came in $180K under, headcount ran $95K over due to a backfill that started early, tooling renewals saved $140K after a vendor renegotiation, and a $325K contingency reserve was released back rather than spent.

Open with the three or four headline figures the room cares about most (total variance, variance percentage, remaining runway), then walk through that movement in the order it happened so the committee can see which category to press on — a plain start-versus-end snapshot won't show them which lever actually moved the number. Close on a one-line recommendation for how the released contingency should be redeployed next quarter.

This is a formal board-style session — keep it tight, assertive, and free of raw spreadsheet detail. Every number on screen should be one the committee will actually ask about.
