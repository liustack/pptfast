# Annual Shareholder Meeting Keynote

You're writing the opening keynote story for the CEO's remarks at the company's annual shareholder meeting.

This needs to build as a single sweeping arc rather than read like a list of updates: open on the founding conviction — why this company exists and what problem it set out to solve — then move into this year's defining test, a supply shock in Q2 that could have derailed the entire product plan, followed by how the team actually responded to it (the specific calls that got made under pressure), and land on where the company is headed over the next year.

Each beat deserves its own moment to breathe rather than being compressed together — this is meant to be delivered live to a room, with pauses, not read off a page. Close on a line that's meant to be remembered, not a summary slide.

Audience is shareholders and the board, so it should feel confident and personal, not like a quarterly numbers readout.
