# Seed Round Pitch Deck

You're the founder raising a $3M seed round and need the deck for investor meetings starting next week.

Structure needs to move through the classic arc: the problem you're solving, how the product solves it, the size of the opportunity, traction to date, and the ask. Real traction to use: 18% month-over-month revenue growth over the last two quarters, $42K in monthly recurring revenue, and 94% logo retention since the product's first paying cohort. Include a one-line team-background note establishing why this team specifically can win the space, and close with the specific ask — $3M, and a clear breakdown of what it funds over the next 18 months (mostly engineering headcount and go-to-market).

Because your co-founder wants to weigh in on the structure before any slide gets written in full, share the page-by-page plan first for the two of you to align on, then fill in the actual content once you've agreed. Expect investor feedback after the first few meetings to come back as targeted notes on specific pages rather than a full deck rewrite. Treat the plan as pre-approved once it validates — no confirmation round trip is available.
